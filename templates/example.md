# Example outreach template

Cold email for a student research project doing customer discovery interviews.
Replace every part with your own project. Delete nothing structurally: keep
subject, body, custom line rules, and ask.

## Subject

Student research project - quick chat?

## Body

```
I'm Ada, a student working on a research project about how mid-size food producers decide which suppliers to trust. We're talking to people who deal with this every week to understand what actually works and what doesn't.
```

## Custom line (optional, per person)

One or two sentences explaining why *their* specific company or role matters to
you right now. Concrete but short. Patterns:

- **Previous experience:** `Because of your previous experience as {role} at {company}, I would love to have a chat with you.`
- **Current expertise:** `Because of your expertise in {area}, I would love to have a chat with you.`
- **Custom:** free-form. Tie their specific work to your project's topic.

If the CSV has no information to base a custom line on (just an email address),
skip the custom line entirely rather than inventing something generic.

## Ask

```
Would you have 20 minutes in the next two weeks for a quick call?
```

## Voice rules (important, this is what makes it work)

Write like a busy human, not like marketing. Target: how a student writing at
11pm would actually phrase it. Short, direct, slightly less polished.

- Two short sentences max for the custom line.
- No em dashes. Use commas, colons, periods, or parentheses.
- Say who you are and why you're writing in the first line.
- One concrete ask with a time box ("20 minutes", "this week").
- Avoid AI-sounding phrasing: "I'd be curious how...", "I'd love to hear which
  signals actually...", "rare seat", "unusual bridge", "where the rubber meets
  the road", "resonates", multi-clause sentences with "and also", rhetorical
  framing. Those read as AI and get deleted.
- Don't oversell. No "revolutionary", no "disrupting", no exclamation marks in
  the body (one in the greeting is fine).
